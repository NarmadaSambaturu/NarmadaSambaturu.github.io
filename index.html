Research Interests

Mathematical and Computational Biology, Systems Biology, Epidemiology, Vaccines and Immunotherapy, Multi-scale Modeling, Immune Response, Host-pathogen Interactions.

I am interested in the study of pathogen evolution, disease progression, and disease spread, with an eye on immune response, and the complex interactions between the molecular, systems, and population scales. I strive to identify important questions in these fields, and develop novel, multi-scale methods to answer these questions. Some of the questions I am interested in are:

  • - How does the host affect pathogen evolution?

  • - What selection pressures drive the evolution of a pathogen?

  • - What has the pathogen evolved to be?

  • - What pathways are involved in a disease?

  • - Can we predict disease outcomes?

  • - How does differential response to a pathogen affect its epidemic potential?

  • - What happens if there are multiple circulating strains?

  • - Can we match patients with the most appropriate therapy, including potential combination therapies?

  • - Can we identify emerging zoonoses, i.e. potential transmission from animals to humans?

  • - Can we predict potential pandemics and prevent them from happening?
